Old Oak Community Events: What's On in West London

By Old Oak Town Editorial  |  June 2026  |  5 min read

Old Oak Common may be a construction site in many people's imaginations, but it is also a living, breathing community where thousands of people go about their daily lives. Cultural events, community meetings, sports clubs, markets and neighbourhood gatherings all take place here — often in the shadow of cranes and site hoardings, but no less vibrant for that. This is your guide to what's happening and how to get involved.

Upcoming Community Events

The following events reflect the kinds of regular happenings in the Old Oak and Park Royal area. Check the Old Oak Town events calendar for the most current listings and confirmed dates.

Monthly

Old Oak Neighbourhood Forum Meeting

Open public meetings where residents discuss planning, development and community issues. A genuine chance to influence what happens here.

Saturdays

Park Royal Artisan Market

Local producers, street food and crafts from the Park Royal community. Growing in popularity as the area's residential population expands.

Summer

Canal Festival (Grand Union)

Annual celebration along the Grand Union Canal towpath — live music, boats, local food and family activities.

Ongoing

Wormwood Scrubs Community Days

Organised litter picks, wildlife walks and outdoor activities on Wormwood Scrubs — one of West London's largest open spaces, right on Old Oak's doorstep.

Community Groups and Organisations

The social fabric of Old Oak Common is woven together by dozens of community groups, many of which predate the regeneration by decades. These are the organisations making things happen.

Old Oak Neighbourhood Forum

The Neighbourhood Forum is a statutory body with formal rights to comment on and influence planning applications in the OPDC area. Its monthly meetings are open to all residents and are one of the most direct ways to engage with decisions about the neighbourhood's future. Meetings typically cover planning applications, OPDC consultations, local infrastructure issues and community projects.

Friends of Wormwood Scrubs

Wormwood Scrubs — the 180-acre open space immediately north of Old Oak — is one of West London's most important green lungs. The Friends group organises conservation work, recreational events and advocacy to protect the Scrubs from development pressures. As Old Oak grows, the Scrubs will become increasingly important as the neighbourhood's primary green space.

Park Royal Business Group

Representing the businesses of Europe's largest industrial estate, the PRBG is a powerful voice in local governance. It runs networking events, skills programmes and advocacy work on behalf of the area's employers — relevant for any resident looking for local employment or business connections.

Get Involved: The best way to stay connected to Old Oak community life is to sign up to The Old Oak Weekly — our free Thursday newsletter covers upcoming events, planning news and community stories every week.

Sports and Leisure

The area has a rich but sometimes hidden sporting life. Football pitches on Wormwood Scrubs are used by local teams throughout the year. The Grand Union Canal provides a corridor for running and cycling. The Powerleague five-a-side centre at Park Royal is a popular venue. And as new residential developments complete, residents' amenities including gyms and communal sports spaces are being added to the neighbourhood's offer.

Cultural Events

Old Oak and Park Royal is one of London's most ethnically and culturally diverse areas — a characteristic that drives a rich calendar of cultural celebration. Eid gatherings, Diwali events, Caribbean heritage celebrations and Polish cultural events all feature in the local calendar, reflecting the extraordinary mix of communities that call this corner of West London home.
