# Content Strategy: Three-Pillar Approach

## Overview
Old Oak Town's content strategy is built on three distinct pillars that work together to create authority, engagement, and trust with the community.

## Pillar 1: Factual & Foundational Content (70% of content)

### Purpose
Serve as the one-stop-shop for official information about Old Oak Common development.

### Content Types
1. **HS2 Construction Updates**
   - Station platform installation progress
   - Tunnel construction milestones
   - Timeline updates and delays
   - Technical specifications
   - Safety records and incidents

2. **OPDC Masterplan Progress**
   - Planning applications approved/rejected
   - Development phase completions
   - Infrastructure investments
   - Public realm improvements
   - Commercial space developments

3. **Public Consultations & Events**
   - Upcoming consultation dates
   - How to participate guides
   - Results and community feedback summaries
   - Event calendars
   - Meeting minutes (where public)

4. **Transport Updates**
   - Elizabeth Line integration news
   - Great Western Railway connections
   - Local bus service changes
   - Cycling infrastructure development
   - Road closures and diversions

5. **Planning Applications**
   - New applications submitted
   - Approval/rejection decisions
   - Appeal outcomes
   - Public comment periods
   - How to object/support guides

### Sources
- HS2 Ltd official announcements
- OPDC website and news feeds
- London Borough of Hammersmith & Fulham
- London Borough of Ealing
- London Borough of Brent
- Construction Enquirer
- Railway PRO
- Building Design
- Local council planning portals

### Publishing Frequency
- Daily: RSS aggregation from sources
- 5-10 articles per week (after HITL review)

## Pillar 3: Deep Dive Analysis (10% of content)

### Purpose
Build authority and trust through in-depth investigative journalism that mainstream media won't provide.

### Content Types

1. **HS2 Delay Analysis**
   - Reasons for timeline shifts
   - Financial impact assessment
   - Comparison with international projects
   - Political factors and decisions
   - Expert interviews and opinions
   - Community impact studies

2. **Financial Challenges Investigation**
   - Budget overruns and explanations
   - Funding sources and changes
   - Value for money assessments
   - Economic benefits projections vs reality
   - Contractor performance analysis

3. **Community Impact Reports**
   - Displacement and rehousing
   - Noise and air quality monitoring
   - Traffic disruption analysis
   - Local business impact surveys
   - Mental health and wellbeing studies
   - Affordability of new housing

4. **Comparative Studies**
   - Similar regeneration projects (King's Cross, Stratford)
   - International case studies
   - Lessons learned analysis
   - Best practice recommendations

5. **Future Scenarios**
   - What if HS2 is further delayed?
   - Alternative uses for Old Oak Common
   - Long-term economic projections
   - Climate resilience of the development
   - Technology integration possibilities

### Sources
- Freedom of Information (FOI) requests
- Parliamentary reports and committees
- Academic research papers
- Industry expert interviews
- Resident surveys and feedback
- Independent consultancy reports
- Data analysis (property prices, demographics, traffic)

## Content Quality Standards

### All Content Must:
1. Be fact-checked against original sources
2. Include proper attribution and links
3. Use clear, accessible language (no jargon without explanation)
4. Include relevant images (properly licensed)
5. Be optimized for SEO (keywords, meta descriptions, headings)
6. Be mobile-friendly
7. Pass HITL review before publishing

### Pillar 1 Specific:
- Source must be verifiable
- No speculation presented as fact
- Dates and numbers must be accurate
- Updates clearly marked if correcting previous info

### Pillar 2 Specific:
- Business information verified directly with business
- Photos must have permission
- Events must have confirmed dates and venues
- Contact information must be current

### Pillar 3 Specific:
- Multiple sources for any claim
- Expert credentials verified
- Data sources publicly available or linked
- Methodology transparent
- Right of reply given to subjects
- Legal review if potentially defamatory

## Crisis Communication Plan

### If We Publish Incorrect Information
1. Immediately update article with correction notice at top
2. Publish separate correction article
3. Notify via newsletter and social media
4. Contact anyone who may have been affected
5. Review HITL process to prevent recurrence

### If Subject of Criticism
1. Respond professionally and factually
2. Provide evidence for claims
3. Offer right of reply to critics
4. Update content if valid concerns raised
5. Maintain transparency about editorial process

### If Legal Threat
1. Consult legal advisor immediately
2. Do not remove content without legal advice
3. Document all communications
4. Review content for accuracy
5. Prepare response with legal guidance
